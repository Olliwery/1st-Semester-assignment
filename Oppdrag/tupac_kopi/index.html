Biography

Tupac Shakur, known as 2Pac, was a highly influential American rapper, actor, and social activist. Born in 1971, he rose to fame with albums like "2Pacalypse Now" and "All Eyez on Me." His music often addressed social and political issues, advocating for racial equality and police brutality awareness.


Beyond music, he pursued acting and appeared in films like "Juice" and "Poetic Justice." 2Pac's life was marred by legal troubles and a feud with East Coast rapper The Notorious B.I.G. Tragically, he was fatally shot in a drive-by shooting in 1996, leaving an enduring legacy in hip-hop and activism, with his message still resonating with fans worldwide.
